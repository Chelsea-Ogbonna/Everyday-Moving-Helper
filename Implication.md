# Implications for the Everyday Moving Helper Design

The primary objective of the Everyday Moving Helper is to provide users with a stable and adaptable platform capable of transporting a variety of cargo types across everyday environments. Unlike traditional carts or robotic cargo assistants with fixed compartments, the proposed platform is intended to carry loads of different sizes and shapes while maintaining stability during movement. As a result, evaluating terrain-induced disturbances is critical because excessive vibration or force fluctuations could cause cargo to shift, tip, or become unsecured during transport.

The force sensor and MPU6050 measurements provide insight into how the platform would perform under realistic operating conditions. While the average force experienced by the platform remained relatively constant across all terrains, rough terrain generated significantly larger force fluctuations and peak loading events. These results suggest that when the platform encounters uneven surfaces, cargo and structural components may experience sudden impact forces. For rigid items such as moving boxes or luggage, these impacts may be tolerable; however, for loosely packed groceries, laundry baskets, or fragile household items, repeated loading events could increase the likelihood of cargo movement or damage.

Similarly, the acceleration measurements obtained from the MPU6050 provide an indication of ride quality and platform stability. Rough terrain produced substantially higher accelerations than both smooth flooring and carpet, demonstrating that terrain irregularities are transferred through the wheels and into the platform structure. Because the proposed design incorporates an expandable deck, these vibrations become particularly important. As the platform expands to accommodate larger loads, the effective footprint increases and the center of mass may shift depending on cargo placement. Elevated vibrations could therefore amplify load movement and reduce overall stability if not properly addressed through structural design and cargo retention mechanisms.

The results also support the inclusion of adjustable side supports within the Everyday Moving Helper. Although the expandable deck increases versatility, it also creates the possibility of transporting tall, unevenly shaped, or loosely packed loads. The increased accelerations observed on rough terrain demonstrate that cargo may experience significant disturbances during operation. Adjustable side panels provide a method for constraining cargo movement and preventing objects from sliding or tipping when subjected to vibration or sudden force spikes.

Furthermore, the moderate performance observed on carpet suggests that the platform is capable of operating across common indoor environments such as homes, dormitories, airports, and office buildings. Carpet generated greater vibration than smooth flooring but substantially less than rough terrain, indicating that the platform should remain stable in many of its intended use cases. This is particularly important for target users such as parents traveling through airports, students transporting belongings across campus, and delivery workers navigating buildings with varying floor surfaces.

The rough terrain results highlight an important design consideration for future development. Since sidewalks, parking lots, and outdoor pathways often contain surface irregularities similar to the tested rough terrain, additional features such as suspension elements, larger wheels, vibration-damping materials, or active control strategies may be required to improve ride quality and cargo security. Incorporating these features would allow the Everyday Moving Helper to better maintain stability while transporting loads across diverse environments.

Overall, the experimental results demonstrate that terrain significantly influences the dynamic behavior of the platform. The findings validate the need for an adaptable footprint, secure cargo retention system, and robust structural design. These characteristics are essential for ensuring that the Everyday Moving Helper can safely and reliably transport a wide range of cargo types across the varied environments encountered in everyday use.
